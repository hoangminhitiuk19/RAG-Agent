guidelines for agronomists: writing effective knowledge templates for ai rag agents objective of these guidelines these guidelines will help agronomists write structured and optimized knowledge templates that can be easily processed, chunked, and retrieved by an ai agent. the ai agent will provide personalized, time-sensitive, and context-aware recommendations to farmers. to ensure accurate and efficient retrieval, follow these writing rules. each rule is designed to make the ai retrieve the right information at the right time without errors or confusion. 1. use plain text with structured headers and bullet points (no tables)  why?

ai models understand text better than tables because tables often lose structure when converted into embeddings. using headers and bullet points ensures the information is stored and retrieved in meaningful, topic-specific chunks. farmers queries are often context-based (e.g., what should i do if my soil is too acidic? ), and a structured text format allows the ai to provide an answer without missing details. how to apply this rule do: write in short paragraphs with clear section headers. use bullet points for step-by-step instructions and lists. group related information together under a single heading.

dont: use tables to present structured data (instead, write it as text). write long, dense paragraphs without subheadings. example (correct way to write it) soil requirements for coffee robusta optimal ph: 5.5 - 6.5 soil type: well-drained, loamy soil with high organic matter. common deficiencies  solutions: acidic soil (5.5 ph): apply agricultural lime. alkaline soil (6.5 ph): use elemental sulfur. sandy soil: increase organic matter with compost and mulch. example (incorrect - using a table)  why is this incorrect? ai might retrieve only one row from the table instead of the full context.

ai cannot reason about cause-and-effect relationships across different table rows. 2. keep each section self-contained (modular structure)  why? ai retrieves small chunks of information at a time. if knowledge is spread across different sections, the ai might retrieve an incomplete answer. writing in standalone sections ensures that the ai can return the right answer even if it only retrieves one part of the document. how to apply this rule each header should introduce a complete idea. do not reference previous sections (e.g., dont say as mentioned aboverepeat the key point).

if a section depends on another, briefly summarize the related information instead of assuming the ai remembers it. example (correct way to write it) how to prevent coffee leaf rust symptoms: yellow-orange powder spots on leaves, leaf drop. prevention: use rust-resistant coffee varieties. prune trees to increase airflow and reduce moisture buildup. apply copper-based fungicides before the rainy season. treatment if already infected: remove and burn infected leaves to prevent spread. apply systemic fungicides if more than 10 of the plant is affected.

example (incorrect - not self-contained) how to prevent coffee leaf rust see the disease management section above for prevention methods. why is this incorrect? the ai may not retrieve the referenced section, leading to incomplete or missing information. 3. use simple, direct language (avoid technical jargon)  why? many farmers do not have a technical background. ai models work best with clear, simple language that does not require interpretation. if a term must be technical, define it immediately. how to apply this rule use simple words: instead of photosynthesis efficiency, say how well the plant makes food from sunlight.

define technical terms in the same sentence where they appear. example (correct way to write it) how to improve soil fertility for coffee trees compost: improves soil structure and nutrients. apply 5 kg per tree, twice a year. nitrogen (n): helps plants grow leaves. if leaves look pale or yellow, apply compost or organic fertilizer. ph balance: soil should not be too acidic or too alkaline. test with a ph kit and adjust if needed. example (incorrect - too technical) soil fertility enhancement using organic amendments organic matter improves cation exchange capacity (cec), leading to enhanced nutrient bioavailability.

excessive nitrogen volatilization occurs if soil buffers are inadequate. why is this incorrect? farmers (and even some agronomists) may not understand complex terminology. ai might misinterpret the technical explanation, leading to confusing or inaccurate responses. 4. include when  where context (make recommendations time-specific)  why? farmers ask time-sensitive questions (e.g., what should i do in february? or what fertilizer should i apply before the rainy season?). ai works better when recommendations include specific timeframes and regional conditions. how to apply this rule mention seasonal timing (apply in the dry season before flowering).

if a recommendation depends on a location, specify the region (e.g., in vietnam, apply before monsoon rains). example (correct way to write it) fertilization schedule for coffee robusta before flowering (march - april): apply phosphorus-rich fertilizers like bone meal to encourage root growth. during berry formation (may - june): apply potassium (k) to improve bean quality. after harvest (december - january): add compost to restore soil nutrients. example (incorrect - no timing context) apply fertilizers regularly to keep plants healthy. use phosphorus for roots and potassium for fruits. why is this incorrect? ai wont know when to apply the fertilizers.

farmers may use the wrong fertilizer at the wrong time. final writing checklist for agronomists  use plain text with headers and bullet points (no tables). write each section as a self-contained chunk (no references to other sections). use simple, direct language (avoid technical jargon or define it immediately). include when  where context for time-sensitive recommendations. write in a way that a farmer can understand without expert knowledge. by following these guidelines, the ai will retrieve and generate the most accurate, context-aware, and actionable responses for farmers!

optimizing documents for semantic chunking by an ai agent to ensure that an ai agent efficiently processes and retrieves relevant information from a document, the document must be structured in a way that supports semantic chunkingbreaking text into meaningful, contextually complete segments. below are best practices for structuring documents optimized for semantic chunking in ai-driven retrieval systems (e.g., rag models, vector search, knowledge retrieval). 1. use a hierarchical structure ai agents perform better when documents have a clear hierarchical structure with logical segmentation. use headings and subheadings to create distinct chunks.

example structure:  main topic  subtopic 1  key concept a  supporting detail  subtopic 2  key concept b  supporting detail best practices:  use markdown syntax and stick to simple formatting - no fancy formatting  each section should be logically independent to allow retrieval without needing excessive surrounding context. ai models favor consistent patterns, so keep formatting uniform. 2. keep chunks concise but complete ai semantic chunking algorithms work best with self-contained yet concise segments. recommended chunk lengths: example (bad chunking  too large): regenerative agriculture improves soil health by increasing organic matter.

organic matter enhances microbial activity, improves soil structure, and increases water retention. one common method is cover cropping, which involves planting crops like legumes and grasses to fix nitrogen and prevent erosion. another technique is compost application, where decomposed organic material is added to enrich the soil. reduced tillage is also beneficial as it minimizes soil disruption, preserving beneficial organisms and preventing carbon loss. example (good chunking  modular  retrievable): definition: regenerative agriculture is a farming approach that enhances soil health by improving organic matter and biodiversity. method 1  cover cropping: planting legumes and grasses to fix nitrogen and reduce erosion.

method 2  compost application: using decomposed organic matter to enrich soil fertility. method 3  reduced tillage: minimizing soil disturbance to preserve beneficial microbes and prevent carbon loss. 6. embed inline definitions  faqs ai models retrieve short answers effectively from faqs and definitions. example (good format - inline definitions  faqs) what is cover cropping? cover cropping is the practice of planting specific crops (e.g., legumes, grasses) to improve soil health, prevent erosion, and fix nitrogen. how does cover cropping improve soil? 1. nitrogen fixation: legumes like clover fix nitrogen, reducing fertilizer dependency. 2. erosion prevention: deep-rooted grasses protect topsoil.
